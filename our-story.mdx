## Where It Started

In 2015, Marcus Kealoha was working the early shift at his family's fish stall at the Honolulu Fish Auction — the same auction his grandfather had run since the 1970s. Marcus grew up eating poke the way it was meant to be: fresh fish, simple seasoning, rice on the side.

After moving to San Francisco for graduate school, he noticed something was missing. Poke had arrived in the Bay Area, but it had become overpriced and disconnected from the ingredient that made it great: the fish itself.

In spring 2017, Marcus borrowed a card table, got a permit, and showed up at the Ferry Building with a cooler of fresh ahi and a bowl of white rice. He sold out in 90 minutes.

---

## Growing Up

For the first 18 months, My Fish Guy was just Marcus, his sister Leilani, and a folding table with a banner that read **"Fresh Poke. Honest Fish."** The menu had three options. Word spread the way the best things spread — through friends, through Instagram, through people telling other people to show up.

By early 2018, the Ferry Building gave Marcus a permanent indoor kiosk. The Mission brick-and-mortar opened in October 2018. That winter, Bon Appétit named it one of the **"10 Best New Restaurants in America."**

---

## Today

My Fish Guy now has **14 locations** across San Francisco, Los Angeles, New York City, and Seattle, with Austin and Denver opening in 2025. Marcus still works the opening shift at the Ferry Building location every Tuesday.
